About this tool (Overview)

The Double Dummy Solver assumes all 52 cards are known and computes the maximum achievable tricks under best play from both sides. It helps you compare contract ceilings by denomination and reassess the best final contract.

How to use

  1. Assign cards to North, South, East, and West until all 52 cards are entered.
  2. Click Analyze to view trick ceilings for NT and each suit contract.
  3. Compare N/S and E/W outcomes to support practical auction and contract decisions.

Glossary

Double Dummy
Optimal-play analysis under full information where all four hands are visible.

About this tool (Overview)

Single Dummy Solver samples unknown hands from visible cards plus HCP and shape constraints, then estimates trick distributions. It is useful in practical situations where complete information is unavailable.

How to use

  1. For North/South, choose Hand or Feature input mode and enter your constraints.
  2. For unknown hands, define HCP ranges, suit-length ranges, and shape type.
  3. After analysis, compare average tricks, game probability, and slam probability.

Glossary

Balanced Hand
A shape of 4333, 4432, or 5332. Whether it includes five major cards depends on the agreed rules.
Semi-balanced Hand
A hand with no shorts or seven-card suits, and typically without a six-card major.

About this tool (Overview)

Opening Lead Analyzer compares candidate opening leads against an assumed contract and reports expected tricks together with set probability. It supports both defensive lead selection and declarer-side risk awareness.

How to use

  1. Set the opening leader seat, contract, and number of simulations.
  2. Enter the leader's exact 13 cards, then set HCP/shape ranges for other seats.
  3. Compare Exp Tricks and Set Prob to choose the most practical opening lead.

Glossary

Balanced Hand
A shape of 4333, 4432, or 5332. Whether it includes five major cards depends on the agreed rules.
Semi-balanced Hand
A hand with no shorts or seven-card suits, and typically without a six-card major.
Set Probability
Probability of defeating the contract by at least one trick.
